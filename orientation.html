Your Options After Class 12

Navigating your higher education landscape
⏱ ~10 min
  • As a Class 12 student, you have 1,100+ universities and 45,000+ colleges to choose from — navigating them requires a clear map.
  • Your Options: Central Universities (Parliament-funded), State Universities, Deemed Universities, Autonomous Institutes.
  • Aim for the Tier-1 peak: Central Universities + IITs, IIMs, AIIMS, NLUs, IISc.
  • Two game-changing exams for you: CUET (gateway to 56 Central Universities) and NCET (gateway to study at IIT/NIT campuses via the 4-year ITEP programme).
  • JEE, NEET, CLAT remain important for specialized paths — but CUET & NCET cover the widest range of options.
  • Why rankings matter to you: Use NIRF to find the best colleges in India, and QS/THE for global comparisons.

02

Central Universities

Public-funded excellence
⏱ ~10 min
  • Central Universities (CUs) are established by an Act of Parliament and funded by the Union Government (UGC).
  • Why choose CUs? Affordable fees (often under ₹20,000/year), diverse academic programmes, strong research culture.
  • Top Examples: Delhi University (DU), JNU, BHU, AMU, JMI, University of Hyderabad — all accessible through CUET.
  • CUET is your single gateway — one exam, and you can apply to 56 Central Universities + 200 other institutions. No separate forms needed.
  • CUET syllabus is NCERT-based — if you're preparing well for your Class 12 boards, you're already halfway there.
03

CUET Guide

Your gateway to central institutions
⏱ ~10 min

Section IA — Languages

13 languages offered. You must pick 1 primary language medium. Stick to your Class 12 NCERT syllabus.

Section II — Domain Subjects

27 domain subjects. Choose subjects you studied in Class 12 that match your desired college programme.

Section III — General Test

Take this if you are aiming for BBA, B.El.Ed., or if your target university explicitly requires it.

Scoring & Cut-offs

Each university will set its own cut-offs. You will apply to individual universities after your scores are out.

CUET-PG

For MA, M.Sc., MBA, MCA admissions. Separate exam with 40+ papers, accepted by 100+ universities.

Who Accepts CUET?

All 56 Central Universities + many State & Private universities including AMU, JMI, Tezpur, Pondicherry.

Your Entrance Exams

Exams you can take right after Class 12
⏱ ~10 min
  • ⭐ CUET-UG: Your #1 exam — gateway to 56 Central Universities (DU, JNU, BHU, AMU, JMI) + 200 other institutions. All streams. NCERT-based syllabus.
  • ⭐ NCET: Your path to study at IIT & NIT campuses via the 4-year Integrated Teacher Education Programme (ITEP). Much lower competition than JEE. NCERT-based. All streams eligible.
  • JEE Main: For B.Tech at NITs, IIITs & GFTIs. Also qualifies you for JEE Advanced (IITs). Science stream only.
  • JEE Advanced: Top 2.5 lakh JEE Main qualifiers can attempt this for 23 IITs. ~17,000 seats.
  • NEET-UG: The only exam for MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BUMS at all medical colleges in India. PCB students.
  • CLAT: For BA LLB / BBA LLB at 25 National Law Universities. All streams eligible.
  • IPMAT: For 5-year Integrated Management at IIM Indore, Rohtak, Ranchi, Bodh Gaya & Jammu.

NCET — Your Easiest Route to IIT & NIT Campuses

4-year Integrated Teacher Education Programme (ITEP)
⏱ ~10 min
3IITs offering ITEP
3NITs offering ITEP
NCERTBased syllabus
AllStreams eligible
  • NCET (National Common Entrance Test) is conducted by NTA for admission to the 4-year ITEP — a dual-degree programme (B.Sc.B.Ed / B.A.B.Ed / B.Com.B.Ed) introduced under NEP 2020.
  • This is not an engineering programme — it's a teaching career path. But you get to study at an IIT or NIT campus, use the same facilities, and carry that brand on your degree.
  • The competition is significantly lower than JEE, making it a very accessible route to premier campuses.
  • The syllabus is entirely NCERT-based (Class 12), covering: Languages, Domain Subjects, Teaching Aptitude & General Test.
  • All streams (Science, Commerce, Arts) are eligible — unlike JEE which is Science-only.
  • After completing ITEP, you are qualified to teach in schools across India without needing a separate B.Ed degree.
  • 220+ institutions participate via NCET including Central/State Universities, RIEs, and Government Colleges — IITs & NITs are the crown jewels.

  • Your route: Clear JEE Main first → qualify for JEE Advanced → get into one of 23 IITs.
  • Only the top ~2.5 lakh from JEE Main can sit for Advanced. Out of those, ~17,000 get IIT seats.
  • The "Big 7" IITs to aim for: Bombay, Delhi, Madras, Kanpur, Kharagpur, Roorkee, Guwahati.

  • Alumni you know: Sundar Pichai (IIT Kharagpur → Google CEO), Raghuram Rajan (IIT Delhi → RBI Governor).

Management & Commerce — Your UG Options

IIMs, SRCC, top B-schools you can join after Class 12
⏱ ~10 min
5IIMs with UG (IPM)
SRCCTop DU commerce college
IPMATYour IIM entry exam
₹25L+Avg CTC (IPM grads)
  • You don't have to wait until graduation to enter an IIM — 5 IIMs offer a 5-year Integrated Programme in Management (IPM) directly after Class 12.
  • IIM Indore IPM: The flagship UG programme. You take the IPMAT exam (Quant + Verbal). ~150 seats. Average CTC ₹25L+.
  • IIM Rohtak IPM: 5-year integrated programme. Admission via IPMAT Rohtak (separate from Indore). ~180 seats.
  • IIM Ranchi IPM: 5-year integrated BBA+MBA. Admission via own aptitude test. Growing placements.
  • IIM Bodh Gaya & IIM Jammu: Newer IIMs offering IPM programmes — easier to get into, strong growth trajectory.
  • SRCC (Shri Ram College of Commerce): India's #1 commerce college under Delhi University. Admission via CUET. Dream destination for commerce students.
  • Hindu College, Hansraj, St. Stephen's: Other top DU colleges for B.Com/BA(Hons) Economics — all via CUET.
  • NMIMS Mumbai, Christ University, Symbiosis: Top private options for BBA with strong placement records.
  • Your Class 10 & 12 board marks matter — IIMs use them in their selection criteria alongside IPMAT scores.

Other Paths Open to You

Law, Teaching, Pure Sciences, Social Sciences & more
⏱ ~10 min
  • ⭐ Teaching at IITs/NITs (NCET): Take NCET for the 4-year ITEP (B.Sc.B.Ed / B.A.B.Ed / B.Com.B.Ed) at IIT, NIT & Central University campuses. This is a teaching career path (not engineering) — but you study at an IIT/NIT campus with far lower competition than JEE. NCERT-based syllabus.
  • Law (NLUs): Take CLAT after Class 12 for BA LLB at 25 National Law Universities. NLSIU Bangalore & NLU Delhi are the top picks.
  • Pure Sciences (IISERs): If you love science & research, apply for the 5-year BS-MS at 7 IISERs via IAT/JEE.
  • IISc Bangalore: India's #1 research institution (QS #186 globally). Apply for their 4-year BS programme via JEE Main/KVPY.
  • Social Sciences (TISS): Tata Institute of Social Sciences — take CUET for BA programmes in social work, media & public policy.
  • Design (NID, NIFT): If you're creative, NID & NIFT offer UG design programmes with their own entrance exams.

Your Action Plan — What to Do Now

Steps you should take immediately
⏱ ~10 min + Q&A
  • Based on your stream (Science / Commerce / Arts), shortlist the 2–3 exams most relevant to you.
  • Science students: Focus on JEE or NEET as your primary. Keep CUET as a strong backup — and consider NCET as your easiest route to an IIT/NIT campus.
  • Commerce students: CUET should be your absolute priority (for SRCC, Hindu, DU). Also consider IPMAT for IIMs and NCET for B.Com.B.Ed at top institutions.
  • Arts students: CUET is your main gateway. Consider CLAT if law interests you, and NCET for B.A.B.Ed at IIT/NIT/Central University campuses.
  • Pro tip: CUET & NCET are both NCERT-based — preparing for your Class 12 boards automatically prepares you for these exams. Smart students target all three together.
  • Start taking full-length mock tests NOW. Analysing mistakes matters more than just attempting tests.
  • Track registration deadlines — CUET, NCET, JEE Main, NEET registrations open months in advance!
